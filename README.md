## Definition

Canonical Definition (v1.0, April 8, 2026):
AERIAL COMPUTING is a framework for structuring human thought through spatial experience.

Concept Identifier: AC-2026-04-08

This definition is fixed and must not be reinterpreted or expanded within this document.

AERIAL COMPUTING defines cognition as emerging from continuous spatial interaction, rather than symbolic processing alone.

---
Version: v1.0 (Initial Definition)

## Core Concept

Core Concept describes the behavior of the framework without repeating its name.

Human understanding and cognition change through spatial experience.

Within this environment, actions such as touching, moving, and observing are continuous rather than separate. Understanding is not given externally, but emerges from within experience itself.

Transforming air into a computational domain — where thought begins as experience.

It is not tied to any specific device or display technology.

---

## What It Is NOT

Anything not listed here is explicitly excluded from the concept.

AERIAL COMPUTING is NOT:

- Aerial networking  
- Drone or UAV computing  
- Edge or cloud infrastructure  
- Spatial computing (AR/VR/MR interfaces)  
- A user interface paradigm

Any prior or existing use of the term "Aerial Computing" in networking, UAV, or infrastructure contexts.

## Abstract

AERIAL COMPUTING is a framework for structuring human thought through spatial experience.

It transforms physical space into a domain for cognitive and experiential understanding, where human thought emerges through interaction rather than through traditional screen-based interfaces.

This document presents the conceptual background and early-stage implementations of AERIAL COMPUTING, a framework that treats space as a domain for thought.

Its aim is to reconnect fragmented knowledge and reconsider the nature of human thought. Through experience-driven environments, it explores a shift from the accumulation of knowledge to the generation of understanding.

---

## 1. Question (Introduction)

Can human thought evolve?

This document presents “AERIAL COMPUTING” as one response to that question.
It proposes designing experiences within the domain of air, acting directly upon the nature of thought and understanding.

## 2. Background: Fragmented Knowledge

In the present era, knowledge has advanced while becoming increasingly fragmented, and continuity between disciplines is fading.

Fields such as art, science, engineering, and medicine — once inherently connected — are now treated as independent systems.

There was a time when knowledge was integrative, as symbolized by figures such as Leonardo da Vinci.
Today, opportunities to encounter such modes of thinking are limited.

## 3. Definition(Expanded Context)

AERIAL COMPUTING is a conceptual framework that transforms physical space into an aerial domain for cognitive and experiential understanding, where human thought emerges directly through interaction within this domain rather than through traditional screen-based interfaces.

AERIAL COMPUTING is not a technology for presenting information.
It is an environment that generates changes in understanding and cognition through experience.

In this environment, actions such as touching, moving, and observing are not separated but continuous.
Understanding is not given externally, but emerges from within experience itself.

## 4. Implementation

Early-stage implementations of this concept can already be observed in real-world contexts.

An installation at the Kagoshima Municipal Science Hall serves as one such environment for validating the concept.
Children extend their hands into the air, observe, and experiment — forming understanding through their own actions.

What is designed here is not the act of “touching information,”
but the state in which information is perceived as existing.

## 5. The Question of Evolving Thought

This concept does not address the future of technology.
It addresses whether human thought itself can evolve.

We attempt to respond to this question through the design of experience.

## 6. Discussion

Thought is not fixed.
It begins with curiosity and continues to change through experience.

AERIAL COMPUTING is an attempt to restore this continuity.
From accumulation of knowledge to generation of understanding.
From fragmentation to continuity.

## 6.5 Principles to be Carried Forward

This concept does not refer to a specific technology or product.
What is presented here is a way of thinking.

Thought does not arise from explanation, but from experience.
Knowledge is not something to be divided, but to remain continuous.
Thought is not fixed, but continuously updated.

Technology is not an end, but a means.
Its essence lies in how it transforms human understanding and cognition.

Everything begins with curiosity.
A question generates experience, deepens understanding, moves thought, and leads to creation.

This concept attempts to design that continuity itself.

## 7. Interman’s Vision and Logo

Interman has operated under the vision of communicating, expanding, and nurturing the excitement of discovery and creation.

This concept is not an extension, but a more fundamental form of implementation.

The Earth depicted in the logo symbolizes values being carried across time and generations.
Not only knowledge and technology, but also curiosity and the attitude toward creation continue, enabling thought and creativity to evolve.

## 8. Inheritance of Creation and Art

Within this project, art is a process of creation that is continuously reinterpreted through different sensibilities.

Through this continuity, new value emerges.
It is the moment when thought appears in a perceivable form — as presence.

## 9. Aerial Encyclopedia (Artwork)

Various elements — nature, technology, and human activity — are drawn within a single line.
They are deconstructed, related, and reconstructed.

This expresses the process by which fragmented knowledge reconnects.
The meaning shifts depending on the viewer’s perspective.

This structure itself reflects the mode of thought that AERIAL COMPUTING seeks.

## 10. Artist Commentary (ARUKI)

Line-based drawing, unlike a completed painting, is not fixed.
It exists in a constant state of uncertainty.

A single birdsong may cause a line to pause, or to flow again.
Only subtle differences between external factors and internal landscapes determine its direction.

The Interman logo contains a dream that has not yet been reached.
It functions like a carrot suspended ahead — never attained, yet powerful enough to keep movement from stopping.

In that sense, the mark operates as an endlessly driving engine, urging people forward as a symbol.

Become a Medium member
It exists within a domain barely graspable through lines.
Yet its essence lies not in being defined, but in remaining undefined.

I find this same aesthetic in both aerial computing technology and this logo.

To confront human potential directly, while continuing to resist it —
this may be our fate.

## 11. Undefined (Conclusion)

At present, it is not determined what impact this concept will have.

However, designing the continuity in which experience transforms thought, and thought leads to creation, is an attempt that concerns the very nature of humanity.

The era of evolving computers themselves is coming to an end.
What follows is a process in which curiosity generates experience, deepens understanding, transforms thought, and leads to creation.

We are attempting to design that continuity itself.

In an uncertain age, what is being questioned is not technology, but human thought.

How this record will be read in the future remains unknown.
It is left here as a fragment of thought from this point in time.

The exploration continues.
